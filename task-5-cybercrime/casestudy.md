# 🛡️ Task 5: Cybercrime Case Study

**Selected Topic:** UPI/Online Payment Fraud (QR Code Scam)

### 🔍 Overview
UPI fraud involving QR codes is a prevalent financial cybercrime where attackers trick victims into scanning a QR code to "receive" money. In reality, the action authorizes a deduction from the victim's account rather than a deposit.

### 📉 Step-by-Step Scenario
1.  **The Hook:** An attacker contacts a student selling an item on a digital marketplace (like OLX), pretending to be a highly interested buyer.
2.  **The Trust Build:** The attacker claims they want to send an advance payment immediately to "book" the item.
3.  **The Trap:** The attacker sends a QR code via a messaging app (WhatsApp) and instructs the victim to scan it to "verify their account and receive the payment."
4.  **The Execution:** As soon as the victim scans the code and enters their UPI PIN, they inadvertently authorize a debit transaction. 
5.  **The Consequence:** Funds are instantly withdrawn from the victim's bank account. Because UPI is a real-time settlement system, the money is often moved to multiple accounts rapidly, making recovery extremely difficult.

### 🎯 Target Audience & Impact
College students and young professionals who are frequent users of digital payment apps are primary targets for this scam. Beyond the financial loss, victims often experience a significant loss of trust in digital banking systems and potential exposure of linked account details.
