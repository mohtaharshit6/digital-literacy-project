# 🛡️ 'Stay Safe Online' Prevention Checklist

As a Student Digital Ambassador, I have compiled these 8 essential tips to help college students in India avoid common cyber threats and financial fraud.

### 💳 UPI & Financial Safety
1. **PIN is for Sending, Not Receiving:** Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money. A UPI PIN is only required to authorize a deduction from your account.
2. **Verify the Merchant Name:** Before hitting 'Pay' on any UPI app, always double-check the verified name displayed on the screen to ensure it matches the intended recipient.

### 🔒 Account & Data Security
3.**Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):** Activate 2FA on your college email, LinkedIn, and GitHub to add an extra layer of security beyond just a password.
4. **Use Strong, Unique Passwords:** Avoid using the same password for your VITyarthi portal and personal social media accounts. Use a mix of symbols, numbers, and cases.
5. **Check for HTTPS:** Before entering any personal details on a website, ensure the URL begins with `https://` and shows a padlock icon in the browser.

### 📱 Social & Browsing Safety
6. **Beware of "Free" Offers:** Do not click on links promising free internship certificates, expensive gadgets, or unverified scholarship rewards.
7. **Public Wi-Fi Caution:** Avoid accessing your bank accounts or sensitive college portals while connected to unsecured public Wi-Fi networks.
8. **App Permissions:** Regularly review the permissions you grant to mobile apps.If an app asks for access to your contacts or gallery unnecessarily, deny it.

---

### 🚨 How to Report Cybercrime
If you or someone you know falls victim to a digital scam, report it immediately to the official Indian authorities:

* **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal:** [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)
* **National Helpline Number:** **1930** (24/7 Toll-Free)
